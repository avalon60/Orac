    Lauren Bacall Dies

    By Scarlett Kilcooley-O'Halloran
    13 August 2014
    Getty Images

    HOLLYWOOD actress and style icon Lauren Bacall has died aged 89.

    Famous for her roles in To Have And Have Not, The Big Sleep, Murder On The Orient Express * and The Mirror Has Two Faces, not to mention her deep, husky voice, * Bacall collected numerous acting nominations and awards throughout her career, including an honorary Academy Award in 2009 for her contribution to "the golden age of motion pictures".

    Bacall got her acting break when she was 19 in 1944, after director Howard Hawks's wife spotted a photograph of the then stage actress in Harpers Bazaar and urged her husband to invite her for a screen test for his film To Have And Have Not. Not only did Bacall get the part of Marie "Slim" Browning, but she met her future husband Humphrey Bogart, who played the lead character Harry Morgan. Bacall and Bogart remained together until his death in 1957. She married her second husband, the actor Jason Robards, in 1961 after a brief romance with Frank Sinatra.

    The actress is survived by her three children Stephen, Leslie and Sam.
